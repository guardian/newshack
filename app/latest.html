Background

The great US government shutdown of 2013 began, said the Iowa senator Tom Harkin, "like a scene from the Hollywood western High Noon". There was the Democrat sheriff Barack Obama, squared off against a gang of southern gunslingers, both sides determined not to give an inch and prepared to blow each other's brains out to get what they came for.

People

Ted Cruz Republican

A first-term Republican senator from Texas and Tea Party favorite. Just before the shutdown, he railed against Obamacare on the Senate floor in a publicity-seeking speech that lasted more than 21 hours.

Barack Obama Democrat

Early on, the president took a position that he would not negotiate at all while the shutdown remained in effect, or while Congress continued to threaten a default. Politically, he had little to lose from severing communications, because they were already blocking the two other things that matter to him - gun control and immigration - and he was therefore even more reluctant to give up his only other big domestic achievement by delaying Obamacare.

John Boehner Republican

The Speaker of the House's weakness as party leader was a major factor in the shutdown. His last-minute deal with vice-president Joe Biden averted the "fiscal cliff" standoff in January, but angered many on the right of his party. This time, he had little support from majority leader Eric Cantor, who is thought to be angling for his job, and Republican whip Kevin McCarthy, who is close to the Tea Party.

Harry Reid Democrat

The Democratic Senate majority leader worked out the final deal with Republican Senate minority leader Mitch McConnell.
